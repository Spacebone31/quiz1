SPORTS

Chess


since 6th century CE

Chess is a board game for two players, called White and Black, each controlling an army of chess pieces, with the objective to checkmate the opponent's king.

Billiard


snooker, cutthroat, straight pool, pool, eight-ball, nine-ball, one-pocket, bank pool, seven-ball, carom billiard, english billiard

Billiards, any of various games played on a rectangular table with a designated number of small balls and a long stick called a cue. The table and the cushioned rail bordering the table are topped with a feltlike tight-fitting cloth.

Soccer


since 3rd century B.C.

It is a team sport, involving 11 players on each side who use their legs, head and torso to pass a ball and score goals. The nature of the game means that players may be sprinting, running fast or slow, and sometimes may be standing around.

Badminton


since 1870s

Badminton is a racket sport played by either two players or pairs who score points by hitting a shuttlecock over the net into the opponent's half. A rally ends when the shuttlecock hits the ground, and each side can hit it only once before crossing the net.
